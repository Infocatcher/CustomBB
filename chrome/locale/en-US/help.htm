Use regular expressions (without brackets). Shielding of a symbol "/" is unessential.
Example:
site1\.com|site2\.com/forum/
That is site1.com or site2.com/forum/
The brief review of regular expressions
| or
. any single character
^ marker of the beginning (for example, ^http://)
$ marker of the ending (for example, \.html$)
m* any quantity of characters "m" (0, 1, ...)
m+ one and more "m" character
m? one or no one
m{2} precisely two
m{2,4} from two up to four (inclusively)
m{4,} four and more
[ab] (only for single characters) "a" or "b"
[^ab] (only for single characters) any character, except "a" and "b"
[a-z] (only for single characters) any character from "a" up to "z"
\d any digit (similarly [0-9])
a(bc|de) "abc" or "ade"
\. point (instead of any character)
